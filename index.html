My passion is incorporating nature into the colors of my yarn. Using marigolds, black walnuts and indigo on locally spun wool. I know that some natural dyers are careful to separate the whole plant matter from the dye before putting in the fiber, but I love the variegated colors that are gained by keeping everything in the mix.

Patterns and Kits. Knitting is the new Yoga!

 

Our kits have everything you need to start knitting: skeins, needles and pattern; this is a great DIY project. Our philosophy is to make knitting patterns that are easy and fun to knit no matter your experience. If you want to help someone get started with crafting, there's no better way than with a kit. They have everything you need to create hats, scarves, cozy blankets, or cardigans! These are not just for beginners. They're a great way of having everything you need at your fingertips!

Our Story A Labor of Love!

 

Have you ever heard of a shepherdess? Look no further than Cambridge, NY. Sue Hayes is the third generation to sheer sheep. Sue is 'little Bow Peep' with a modern twist. Sue has a give and receive relationship with her flock, as do a few members of her community. "You stratch my back and I'll shave yours." The sheep are taken to a local solar farm to help maintain the grass. After her beauties are sheered, the wool is then brought to the Battenkill Fiber Mill, where I pick out my wool. We are three women working separately to create a whole product.
